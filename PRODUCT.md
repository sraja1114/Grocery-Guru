# Product

## Register
product

## Users
Household grocery shoppers planning a weekly shop, typically on a phone or laptop before leaving for the store. They already know what they need; the question is where to buy it. Primary job: confirm which of Walmart, Kroger, or H-E-B has the lowest total for their specific list — fast, without manually checking prices on three apps.

## Product Purpose
GroceryGuru takes a grocery list and compares prices across Walmart, Kroger, and H-E-B. It shows prices per item, highlights the cheapest option at each store, and recommends the store with the lowest total. Current data is mock; future versions will pull live prices. Planned features include saved lists, shopping history, and personalized alerts.

## Brand Personality
Precise, confident, useful. The tone is a knowledgeable friend who knows the prices — not a coupon clipper site, not a corporate aggregator. The tool gives a clear answer and shows its work. It respects the user's time: type your list, get your answer, go shop.

## Anti-references
- Coupon-clipper sites (RetailMeNot, Honey): cluttered, ad-heavy, low-trust design
- Big-box store apps (Walmart.com, Kroger app): corporate color overload, overwhelming SKU density
- Generic SaaS dashboards: identical card grids, sidebar nav, enterprise-neutral gray
- Food delivery urgency (DoorDash, Instacart): high-energy red/orange, impulsive-purchase tone

## Design Principles
1. The answer first. The recommendation is the product; detail is for those who want it.
2. Real names, real stores. Walmart blue, Kroger red, H-E-B red — no abstraction.
3. Precision builds trust. "$3.49" earns confidence that "lower prices" never does.
4. The landing explains; the tool delivers. Two distinct surfaces, two distinct jobs.
5. Promise only what exists. Coming-soon features are acknowledged quietly, not marketed.

## Accessibility & Inclusion
WCAG AA minimum. Color is never the sole indicator of "best price" — checkmarks and labels reinforce color signals. Full keyboard navigation. Reduced motion respected via prefers-reduced-motion. Placeholder text must meet 4.5:1 contrast, not the muted-gray default.
